The Three Months

June Prep - July Submissions - August Finals

Three months instead of one. Born from the working session with Jordan (Empire Builder) - the original "all-in-June" plan compressed too much. The new shape gives builders a month to absorb context, a month to ship, and a month for the Finals to breathe.

June - Prep Month

ZAO Teachers + Vibe Coding Instructors

Recorded sessions, Far-Hack style. ZAO teachers cover governance, Respect, ZOLs, fractals. Vibe coding instructors walk through Claude Code, Cursor, MCP, and agent harnesses. Tool specialists drop tutorials on Empire Builder V3 (with Jordan), zlank.online, POIDH bounties, Juke, and Songjam. Watch live or after. The ZABAL/ZAO/WaveWarZ context skill goes live so any agent can load full ecosystem context with one drop.

July - Submissions Month

Anyone with the chops ships a build aligned with ZABAL, ZAO, or WaveWarZ. Live URL + open-source GitHub repo + 60-second demo + cast on /zabal. Mentors watch rolling and claim champions as builds drop - first-come-first-served per mentor.

August - The Finals

The mentor-champion pairs lock. Same Finals prompt for the whole cohort. 24h build with mentor embedded as teammate + 24h promote + 24h ZAO governance vote + live reveal stream. What This Actually Is

The Why

ZABAL Games is not a generic hackathon. It is a Farcaster-creator onboarding event for the ZAO ecosystem - a structured way to bring hungry, Farcaster-active vibe-coders into ZAO by having them build something real, in public, with a ZAO mentor in their corner.

The core thesis

Bring talented Farcaster builders together. Give them deep context on everything ZAO is building (ZAO OS, WaveWarZ, ZAO Festivals, and the rest). Let them create something that helps the community, with a ZAO mentor as their embedded teammate. Earn governance in the community during the event itself. If they want to keep building with ZAO after - great. If not, they still walked away with a real artifact, a real relationship, and a real onchain credential.

What success looks like

New builders who understand ZAO from the inside. Real artifacts shipped for the ecosystem. A recurring format (this is Season 1) that compounds. The competition is in getting selected through Phase 1 - once you are a Finalist, the Finals are a collaboration, and all finalists win.

Focused on the creator, not the thing they create

The builds matter, but the people matter more. This is about who you are as a builder, how you work in public, how you collaborate with a mentor - not just whether the demo is polished. That is why the application is itself a build (Phase 1): we want to see how you actually ship.

Who Decides

WaveWarZ-Base + Respect Baseline

Decision locked by Doc 720: ZABAL Games Season 1 Finals are settled by the WaveWarZ-Base prediction market protocol (Sam + Arthur ship the Base contracts in June, mainnet July, real Finals August). Not pure 1-person-1-vote, not pure market. A hybrid that combines price discovery with a Respect-holder baseline floor.

How settlement works

Each Finalist becomes a tradable battle entry on WaveWarZ-Base. ZAO members with Respect above a threshold (snapshotted at T+0) get pre-funded positions (~$1-2 USDC each) - this is the baseline allocation that keeps the floor honest for valuable-but-niche builds. The market trades for 72 hours; anyone can buy in alongside the Respect baseline. At T+72h the market settles. Final ranking combines market price with autonomous judge-agent rubric scores (judges are x402-paid agents that read the deployed URL + repo + demo video and emit scored evaluations). Live settlement on /finals/live. Top 3 get the Champion NFT (Hats Protocol on Base). All Finalists get the Finisher NFT. USDC distributed onchain via 0xSplits.

Tooling + Visibility

Use Any Harness - Show Your Work

We don't care if you use Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, Aider, Cline, Bolt, v0, Lovable, or hand-roll your own pipeline. What we care about is that you build in public. Pick at least one primary visibility mode below. Mix and match welcome.

Mode 1

Live Twitch Stream

Stream the bulk of your build live on Twitch. The default - what we recommend. ZAO restreams every finalist to a single hub page so viewers can switch between players.

Mode 2

Recorded Screen Sessions

Screen-record your build sessions and upload to YouTube/Loom within 1h of each session ending. Cast the link to /zabal as you go. Less drama than live but easier on shy builders.

Mode 3

Public AI Prompt Logs

Share your AI conversation logs publicly as you go. Cursor + Windsurf composer history, Claude Code transcript dumps, Aider session logs - whatever your tool exports. Post snippets every 1-2 hours.

Mode 4

Frequent Build Casts

Cast progress on Farcaster every 1-2 hours with screenshots, snippets, and what you're prompting. Lower bar than streaming, higher cadence required. Tag /zabal + @bettercallzaal.

The rule: at least one primary visibility mode, plus ongoing /zabal casts Voters need to verify you actually built it in 24h with AI tools - that's the only constraint. How you show your work is up to you. Pick the mode that fits your personality and ship.

FAQ

Common Questions

How does the two-phase model actually work? +
Phase 1 is open. Anyone can apply by building something with the ZAO context prompt (released when applications open). Use any vibe-coding harness. Ship publicly with a live URL, GitHub repo, demo video, and a cast on /zabal. As builds ship, ZAO mentors watch rolling - each claims one champion (first-come-first-served per mentor). Phase 2 is the Finals - the mentor-player pairs build for 24h, promote for 24h, and the ZAO DAO votes on builds in a separate 24h window. All finalists win - the competition is in Phase 1 (getting selected), the Finals are a collaboration.
Who is this for? +
Anyone willing to build in public + ship a real artifact for Phase 1. Phase 1 is genuinely open to everyone with the chops to ship. ZAO mentors then pick their champions based on whose Phase 1 builds resonate with each mentor's search style. You don't need to be a senior engineer - vibe-coding harnesses close a lot of skill gaps. You DO need to be willing to show your work in public.
What does ZAO actually do for me as a creator? +
Three things. First, distribution - ZAO accounts (BCZ Farcaster, /zao channel, COC Concertz, ZAO OS) amplify every finalist during the promote window so your audience grows whether you place or not. Second, infrastructure - we set up your Twitch/StreamElements onboarding, give you a pre-funded Privy wallet, and provide an Empire Builder API key. Third, content engine - the streaming flywheel auto-clips your build into shorts for TikTok/Reels/YouTube/Farcaster, so 24h of streaming becomes ~20 pieces of content you can use forever.
Do I have to use Claude Code? +
No. Use whatever vibe-coding harness you want - Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, Aider, Cline, Bolt, v0, Lovable, even hand-roll your own pipeline. The constraint is "show your work" not "use this specific tool." Pick one of the 4 visibility modes (live stream, recorded sessions, public prompt logs, or frequent build casts) and ship publicly.
Do I have to stream the entire 24h build? +
Live streaming is just one of four visibility modes. You can instead record screen sessions and upload to YouTube within 1h, share public AI prompt logs, or cast frequent screenshots every 1-2 hours. Pick what fits your personality. Required: at least one primary mode plus ongoing /zabal casts throughout. Voters need to verify you built it in 24h with AI tools - HOW you show your work is up to you.
Can I work as a team? +
Solo only for Season 1. Teams of 2 considered for a future Season. The point is to see what one builder can ship in 24 hours.
Who picks the winners? +
ZAO members who have earned Respect through fractal participation. Respect is ZAO's soulbound, peer-validated reputation - earned in weekly fractal sessions over time. The eligible voter set naturally skews toward long-tenure contributors. Voter set is snapshotted at T+0 so it's locked before the Games begin. 1-person-1-vote mechanism, NOT token-weighted (so a whale holding lots of ZABAL has no extra power - this is governance by earned contribution, not by token holdings). The people deciding are the people who've actually built ZAO and know what's worth building next.
What if my time zone is bad for PT? +
Season 1 is anchored to Pacific Time. We know this disadvantages EU/Asia. A future Season may run regional variants. For Season 1: if you're up for coding through your night, you're up for it. Otherwise wait for the next Season.
Who owns the code I ship? +
You do. Open-source mandate (MIT or similar permissive license) is a submission requirement. ZAO doesn't claim IP. We can fork or extend per the license, but ownership stays with you.
What happens after the games? +
All finalist builds live on as open-source repos in the /zabal Hall of Fame. Some get organically picked up by ZAO ecosystem brands. Some die. Some you keep evolving. No obligation either way - you finished, you got the collectible, you're an alumnus.
Can I read the full spec? +
Yes - Doc 630 in the ZAO research library covers everything: format, prompt design, judging, prize, infra, risks, and strategic considerations. This page is the marketing summary; the doc is the source of truth.
