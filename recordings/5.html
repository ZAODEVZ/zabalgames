Building Sopha, and why curation is needed.

Chris (chriscocreated) and Zaal · Sopha · Builder track - live workshop, recorded

Watch on YouTube Read the transcript Sopha
Chapters

A ZABAL Gamez workshop with Chris (chriscocreated), founder of Sopha - a Farcaster client built for depth and high signal - on what he built and why curation is needed. Sopha is an intentional third place, a coffee shop or library for the deeper writing, art, and thinking that scrolls past on the main feed, with a curated feed kept by the community's curators plus BradQ's The Pulse. Chris traces why he built it (when Farcaster felt like it was tipping into meme coins, he set out to protect the culture of interesting thinkers, and good vibe coding made him the one to do it), how easy it now is to build your own client on Farcaster's open data with Neynar and Hypersnap, and the nested stack of client, mini apps, and Snaps. He closes on where Sopha is headed: a second round of Purple funding, experiments in rewarding curators, infra, and live cultural-center experiences, with a thoughtfully designed, non-extractive token only when the time is right.

Key takeaways
  • Curation is the point - Sopha is a Farcaster client for depth, an intentional third place for high-signal writing and art, not a replacement for the daily feed.
  • Building your own client is now a few hours of work: Farcaster's open data plus Neynar (and Hypersnap) let anyone shape their own view.
  • You are not beholden to the algorithm - you build your own lens. That is the unlock for artists, writers, and creators.
  • The nested stack: a client is just a website showing Farcaster data; below it sit mini apps, and Snaps you can spin up in seconds.
  • The feed is curated by community curators (earn the role by showing taste), and BradQ runs a dedicated Pulse feed.
  • Sopha's next year (a second Purple round): reward curators, cover infra, run live cultural-center experiences, and design any token carefully and non-extractively.
  • Find it: @chriscocreated and @sopha on Farcaster, sopha.social.
