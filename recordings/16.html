ZABAL GAMEZ Workshop w/Dan Singjoy from Eden Fractal

Dan Singjoy · @dansingjoy · Eden Fractal · Builder track - Conversation, recorded

Technical4/10Some technical depth
Watch on YouTube Read the transcript Eden Fractal

Dan Singjoy, founder of Eden Fractal, breaks down the fractal ecosystem - a way for communities to make decisions together that are fair, fast, and fun, using Respect earned through weekly contributions and peer consensus. Dan shares his path from musician to building onchain, founding Eden Fractal and the Eden Creators media studio, and the Optimism Fractal origins where he and Zaal first met. He walks through how a weekly fractal runs, the tripartite governance model, claiming Respect onchain, and how ZAO Fractal now runs itself with as few as four people. It closes on a token-gated Fractal browser idea for community spaces.

Key takeaways
  • A fractal makes group decisions fair, fast, and fun - Respect earned through weekly contributions and peer consensus.
  • Respect is soulbound and illiquid - tied to governance, not tradable.
  • ZAO Fractal now runs itself with as few as four people, no host needed.
  • Respect unlocks governance, funding, and role allocation - not just a score.
  • A token-gated Fractal browser could give a community its own gated workspace.
