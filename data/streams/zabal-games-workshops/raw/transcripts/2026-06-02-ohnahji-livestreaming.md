## Key points

- **Do not abandon what already works.** Chasing the newest tech is a trap. Ohnahji and
  Zaal moved their show from audio Twitter Spaces to live video; most of the Web3-native
  streaming platforms they tried no longer exist, while the proven platforms still give
  real distribution.
- **Consistency is king.** Ohnahji is on day 288 of a 365 daily-stream journey. The first
  week and first month are frustrating for everyone, and that is the point - a daily streak
  forces you to show up and get better. Most creators do not talk about those early days.
- **It is live, so things will break.** Every broadcast tool (OBS, Restream, StreamYard)
  glitches; the internet cuts out. Drop the perfectionism and keep going - chats are used
  to it.
- **Build up to multistreaming.** Sending to multiple platforms strains your connection
  and bitrate and limits what you can show. Add complexity over time, by doing it.
- **Use the platform's community tools.** Twitch raids branch communities together and are
  a discovery engine; Stream Together / Knock lets two streamers share a chat and
  collaborate live. Gamified leaderboards and achievements are genuinely motivating.
- **You can build your own tooling.** You are not locked into Nightbot or StreamElements -
  you can cook up your own chat bots, and (builder-track crossover) Ohnahji is learning to
  prompt-code his own.
- **Streaming is human-to-human connection.** As AI and socials thin out real interaction,
  people long for proof there is a human on the other side. The live chat is the unspoken
  hero of the medium.
- **Security carries over from Web3.** API keys are secrets authenticated to your account;
  keep them in env files and never publish them. Treat your screen like a wallet - anyone
  can clip, freeze, and rewind a stream, so a leaked secret on screen is permanent.
- **When in doubt, slow down.** Nine times out of ten nothing better happens because you
  rushed, and rushing is exactly where security mistakes happen. Take the step back and do
  your due diligence.
