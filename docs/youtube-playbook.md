# YouTube playbook - ZABAL Gamez

> How we use YouTube for the workshop library. Research-backed (2026), tailored to what we are
> doing: turning recorded ZABAL Gamez sessions into a discoverable, bingeable library that
> feeds the build event. Brand rules apply: no emojis, no em dashes (hyphens only), no
> web3/crypto jargon in public copy. Per-video metadata format lives in
> `docs/recordings/youtube-package-template.md`.

## The strategy in one line
Publish each session as a fully-optimized long-form video, connect them with playlists, and
cut the sharpest moments into Shorts that pull new viewers back to the long-form and the build.

## What to do on every upload (the checklist)
1. **Title** - `ZABAL GAMEZ Workshop - [topic] - w/ [presenter]`. The brand stays first for
   consistency; make sure the topic carries a real search keyword (e.g. "Farcaster protocol",
   "legal entities for DAOs"), because that is what people search.
2. **Description** - first two lines are the hook and carry the keyword (they show above the
   fold and in search). Then context, "What you'll take away", chapters, links, the ZABAL
   Gamez boilerplate, and 3-5 hashtags. Full shape in the template.
3. **Chapters** - real transcript timestamps, descriptive names (never "Part 1"). Videos with
   chapters get more watch time because viewers jump to what they want. First chapter at 00:00.
4. **Tags** - 8-12, broad + niche. Tags carry less weight now than title/description, but still
   help with spellings and related terms. Do not stuff.
5. **Thumbnail** - one consistent branded template: presenter face (with expression), 3-4 word
   topic in big high-contrast text, small ZABAL GAMEZ mark. The thumbnail is the single biggest
   click factor - keep it clean, no clutter.
6. **Playlists** - add the video to its playlists on upload (see below). Playlists are the most
   underused SEO lever and they drive session watch time.
7. **End screen** - subscribe + the next workshop + the track playlist (move viewers to the next
   thing, not just the next view).
8. **Cards** - link the project/site mentioned, and a related session, at the moment it comes up.
9. **Pinned comment** - the RSVP/collectible CTA + the presenter's links + zabalgamez.com.
10. **Shorts** - cut 2-3 vertical Shorts from the sharpest moments (we have transcripts to find
    them). Strong hook in the first second, clear topic, and a line/pin pointing to the full video.

## Playlists to create (do this once, then file each video)
- **ZABAL Gamez Workshops** - the whole Season 1 library, newest or in-order.
- **Artist track**, **Builder track**, **Creator track** - one per track, so people who care
  about one lane can binge it.
- Topic playlists where we have a cluster: **Farcaster** (Cassie, Jub Jub, AMA), **Music + artists**
  (WaveWarz, Joseph Goats, Theresa), **Agents + DAOs** (MIDAO, Empire Builder, Bonfire).
- Each playlist gets a keyword-rich title + a 1-2 sentence description of what you will learn.

## Shorts strategy
- Treat Shorts as top-of-funnel, not leftovers. 2-3 per session.
- Pull the moment with a self-contained hook (a sharp claim, a number, a "here is how").
  Examples we already have: Hurricane Ike "earn by engagement, not streams"; Cassie "build a
  client in a weekend"; Aziz "build in public".
- End each Short with a verbal + pinned pointer to the full workshop.

## Cadence
- Publish on a predictable rhythm (the recordings land through June). Consistency teaches the
  algorithm and the audience when to expect drops.
