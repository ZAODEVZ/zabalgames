## 2. What the score is actually saying

RETHINK at 7.2 is **not "weak idea"** - three of four dimensions are strong (clarity 8.0, pain
7.5, channel 7.0). The gate is held down by **unproven assumptions**, headlined by one:
*pull-demand from cold builders is unproven until the 72h experiment returns.* The fastest way

## 3. Challenges to the assessment

FounderCheck is a general validator; a few of its flags under-read a *community-native* product.
These are the rebuttals - and exactly what evidence would update each.

1. **"Pull-demand from cold builders unproven."** Fair as a *cold* test, but it under-weights the
   warm-demand we have already proven: workshop supply is volunteering unprompted, 90+ weeks of
   sustained community participation (retention), and a FarHack win (builder pull). The model is
   **warm-pull-first by design** (ZAO orbit + alumni); cold-pull is upside, not the core thesis.
   *Updates the score:* the 72h cold-DM test hitting 10/30, AND June workshop attendance/retention.
2. **Conversion friction 6.5 / "is 3 months too long?"** This reads the *arc* as the *ask*. The
   actual first ask is a **free 30-minute workshop, recorded, no commitment** - near-zero friction
   - and you can enter at any door (watch in June, or just build in July). *Updates the score:*
   reframing the funnel so the tiny first ask is what a cold visitor sees first (see section 5).
3. **"Ongoing-economics pitch vs larger cash prizes elsewhere is untested."** Partly a category
   error: the target customer is explicitly **not** the prize-maximizer ("not weekend trophies").
   Bigger-cash venues select for a mercenary segment that churns; ZABAL self-selects for
   meaning + stake + distribution. *Updates the score:* a cold builder choosing ZABAL *after*
   being shown both - which the DM test approximates.
4. **Willingness-to-pay "inferred."** Builders are paid, not paying - so the right currency is
   **their time**. A workshop RSVP that converts to attendance is the real willingness-to-spend
   signal. Treat signups + attendance as the WTP proxy, not dollars.

Net: the honest gate is "prove warm-pull converts and cold-pull exists." Both are days away, not
a rebuild.

## 4. The experiment, sharpened

- **Action:** 30 personalized cold DMs to FarHack/Agentic-Bootcamp alumni not in ZAO orbit.
- **Ask:** one free 30-min workshop (RSVP), recorded. Never "commit 3 months."
- **Lead with the differentiator:** real community + ZAO mentor + ongoing upside (a cut, Respect,
  distribution) - the thing that beats a bigger one-time prize.
- **Personalize line 1** with one true detail about their build (the single biggest conversion
  lever).
- **Success:** 10/30 signups (33%). Track via a `form_source: cold-dm` tag on the join/RSVP flow
  so cold conversions are cleanly separable from warm.
- **Read the outcome:** >=10 = cold pull-demand proven (gate -> GO candidate). 4-9 = pitch works
  but channel/targeting needs tuning. <4 = the cold pitch is not landing; the model stays warm-first.

## 5. Move the 6.5 (conversion friction) on-site

- A one-line objection-killer near the hero: "Free. Any harness. Enter at any door - watch one
  workshop, or build in July." (kills "3 months too long").
- Surface **ongoing economics** ("keep a cut + Respect + distribution") in "What you walk away
  with" - it is the differentiator and is currently buried.
- Make the primary CTA the *tiny* ask (RSVP a free workshop); July is secondary.

## 7. For Jonathan / the Farcaster Batches workshop (teaching writeup)

ZABAL Gamez is a clean worked example for teaching FounderCheck to a builder audience. The arc:

1. **A "RETHINK" is a map, not a verdict.** 7.2 with three strong dimensions means "de-risk one
   assumption," not "go back to the drawing board." Teach reading the *why* behind the gate.
2. **Know which validation you are missing.** Distinguish **warm-pull** (your existing community
   wants it - here: proven by 90+ weeks, volunteering supply, FarHack) from **cold-pull** (do
   strangers want it - here: the 72h test). Many founders conflate the two and either over-claim
   or panic. Name which one your gate is actually blocked on.
3. **Your customer is not always the prize-maximizer.** When the product self-selects for
   meaning/ownership over cash, benchmarking against bigger-cash competitors is a category error.
   Teach segment-fit before competitor-fear.
4. **Currency depends on the customer.** For "builders are paid, not paying," willingness-to-pay
   is willingness-to-spend-*time*. Pick the right proxy metric (RSVP -> attend) instead of forcing
   a dollars frame.
5. **Design the cheapest experiment that moves the gate.** 30 DMs in 72h, one clear pass/fail
   number (10 signups), tagged so the data is clean. That is the template: smallest test that
   flips the single blocking assumption.

A builder watching this leaves with: how to read a viability gate, how to challenge it with the
right evidence, and how to design a 72h experiment that earns the next point.
