- 30 personalized cold DMs to FarHack / Agentic-Bootcamp alumni **not** in the ZAO orbit.
- Ask the tiny thing: one free 30-min recorded workshop (RSVP). Never "commit 3 months."

That single run produces the Reach evidence AND the Ease-of-sale evidence AND (with the

## The three capture instruments to set up BEFORE you send a single DM

These are what is missing today. Without them you will run the test and have no clean
evidence to feed back in, and the score will not move even if the test succeeds.

### 1. Cold-conversion proof (raises Ease of sale 6.5 -> 8.5+)

FounderCheck wants "a builder who committed cold." The instrument already exists - the
`source: cold-dm` tag via `/api/join`. You just have to **capture the moment**:

- When a `source: cold-dm` signup lands from someone with zero prior ZAO relationship,
  screenshot it (handle + timestamp) and log it in the tracker's Notes.
- One clean cold join is the proof. Two or three is unarguable.
- Keep it honestly separated from warm signups - a warm signup dressed as cold is the one
  thing that would discredit the whole result.

### 2. Verbatim pain quotes (raises Problem severity off the 7.5 ceiling)

Severity only moves on real quotes, never assertions. Bake one open question into the DM
thread, AFTER they have replied to the ask (so it does not read as a survey):

> "Out of curiosity - after your last hackathon ship, did the momentum carry, or did it
> just kind of die? Trying to figure out if that is a me-thing or an everyone-thing."

Capture the answers verbatim. You want 3-5. Ranked by score-impact:

- Best: someone who **abandoned or walked away** from a bigger-prize program because it
  gave them nothing lasting. That is "left a competing thing" - it lifts severity hardest.
- Strong: a concrete loss - "I spent three weekends, won, and the repo is dead."
- Useful: the emotional version - "you win, then you disappear with nothing."

Drop the raw quotes into a `## Pain quotes (verbatim)` block in the experiment doc as they
come in. Do not paraphrase - the rawness is the credibility.

### 3. External-channel recruit (raises Reach 7.5 -> 8.5+)

This is the piece FounderCheck explicitly asked for and that no current doc covers: **one
channel that pulls a stranger without you in the loop.** Two cheap ways to manufacture it:

- **Workshop-lead referral.** Ask each confirmed lead to recruit ONE builder they have
  never worked with, with a pre-written line and a tagged link `?src=lead-ref-<handle>`.
  If a stranger signs up through a lead, that is a channel that works without Zaal.
  - Lead-referral line (paste-ready, leads personalize the first clause):

    > "[name] - I am teaching a session at ZABAL Gamez (The ZAO's build event). You shipped
    > [thing] - you would fit this. Free, one recorded workshop to start, you keep what you
    > build and a cut of what it earns. Want the link?"

- **Reshare attribution.** When a FarHack alum reshares the `/zabal` announce, point that
  reshare at `?src=reshare` and watch for a cold join off it.

Even ONE stranger converting through a lead or a reshare (not through Zaal) is the proof
FounderCheck wants for Reach.

## Customer clarity 8.0 -> 9.0 (the one thing that is copy, and is honest)

Narrower is clearer. Pick ONE beachhead and lead with it in the pitch and the DMs:

> "Farcaster Agentic Bootcamp alumni who shipped an agent or a mini app and then had no
> users, no distribution, and no crew to keep building with."

This is the only score lever that copy alone moves, because clarity is literally about how
sharply the customer is named - and it is already your strongest dimension, so it is a
cheap half-point.

## What will NOT move the score (stop here if you catch yourself doing these)

- Polishing the share casts, the hero line, or the embed card. That is marketing
  optimization on an untested foundation. Good to have, but it is not validation.
- Re-pitching FounderCheck with better words. It is evidence-gated - it will hold at ~7.2
  and tell you to TEST FIRST again, correctly.
- Padding the pool number, or marking July dropout "solved." Those are tested, not stated,
  and claiming them would backfire.

The whole kit reduces to one sentence: **run the 30 DMs, capture three things, feed back
numbers.** That is the next point.
