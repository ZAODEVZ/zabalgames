## The sharpest move first: route finalists to retroactive builder grants

The highest-leverage way to grow effective builder comp is not always a deposit into our pool -
it is pointing ZABAL Gamez builders at grant programs that pay shipped builders directly.
**Base Builder Grants** is the cleanest fit: a retroactive program (no formal application -
recipients are found via ecosystem activity + community nominations), 1-5 ETH per builder, for
live products and even for "workshops and ecosystem education." A ZABAL Gamez build that ships a
live MIT Base/Farcaster tool is exactly the profile, and the event itself is nominatable. This
multiplies builder upside without us holding the funds. (Source: Base Get Funded docs +
Base Builder Grants on Gitcoin.)

So the strategy is two-track: (A) grow our own pool via sponsors, and (B) be a funnel that gets
our builders into retroactive grant programs and nominates the strongest ones.

## Honest notes per the no-fabrication rule

- **Amounts and rounds change.** Base's $2M pool is a 2025-2026 figure; Gitcoin GG24's QF window
  ran Oct 14-28 2025 with evaluation into 2026. Confirm the current round + dates on each
  program's site before pitching - do not quote a stale number to a prospect.
- **Retroactive grants fund builders, not events.** Base Builder Grants and RetroPGF pay shipped
  builders; framing them as "sponsor our pool" is wrong. The honest pitch is "nominate our
  builders / point our builders at you," which is still a real win for everyone.
- **The relationship belongs to ZAO Festivals.** Route every reply to info@thezao.com; do not
  commit dollar tiers, ROI, exclusivity, or tax status (see `docs/sponsor-outreach-2026-05-26.md`).
- **QF is a fit because of shape, not size.** ZABAL Gamez produces many small open-source builds
  with a real contributor community - the exact thing quadratic funding rewards. That is a
  structural advantage worth leaning on for Gitcoin/Giveth specifically.
