## The short version

FounderCheck took ZABAL Gamez from a **3.35 BLOCK** to a **7.8 TEST FIRST** across three
runs - and, just as usefully, it refused to inflate past that until we run a real
cold-demand test. The number going up taught me one thing; the number refusing to go up
taught me a more important one.

| Run | Score | Gate | What it actually did for me |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 - first run | 3.35 | BLOCK | Caught that my materials read as the **wrong product** |
| 2 - after reframe + evidence | 7.2 | RETHINK | Named the **one** assumption blocking the gate and designed the test for it |
| 3 - after shipping its fixes | 7.8 | TEST FIRST | Ease of sale 6.5 -> 8.0 once I surfaced the tiny first ask on-site; now parked until the cold test returns |

That is a +4.45 swing (3.35 -> 7.8), but the swing is not the point - what each gate taught me is.

## Update 1: the BLOCK that was secretly the most useful read

The first run scored ZABAL Gamez as a **SaaS for vibe-coder game distribution** - customer
"indie game devs," problem "can't distribute vibe-coded games," a $0-15/mo payer funnel. It
BLOCKED at 3.35. My first reaction was "the tool scored the wrong thing."

It had - but that was the signal. ZABAL Gamez is The ZAO's 3-month build event, an
onboarding funnel, not a subscription tool. If an agent fed my own materials concluded
"game distribution tool for vibe coders," then humans reading the same materials would draw
the same wrong conclusion. **The BLOCK was not wrong about the product - it was right about
my clarity.** It surfaced a brand-clarity problem I would not have caught from inside. That
is the most valuable thing a validator did for me, and it came from the score I almost
dismissed.

Fix: I rewrote the one-line framing so the product reads as what it is (event + onboarding
funnel, builders are paid not paying), and re-ran it against the correct description.

## Update 2: the RETHINK that gave me a map instead of a verdict

Fed the right product and real evidence, it landed at **7.2, RETHINK**, with:

- Problem severity 7.5
- Customer clarity 8.0 (strongest)
- Reach strategy 7.0 -> 7.5 on the latest read
- Ease of sale 6.5 (lowest)

The thing I want you to know it did well: a RETHINK at 7.2 with three strong dimensions is
**not** "weak idea." It is "de-risk one assumption." It told me precisely which one:
**pull-demand from cold builders is unproven.** Everything scoring well - 100+ weeks of
weekly Respect, 65+ repos, volunteering workshop leads, the FarHack Snap win - is warm:
people already in the ZAO orbit. FounderCheck would not credit strangers until strangers
showed up. And it would not let me talk the number up - it held at TEST FIRST.

That refusal is the feature. It kept me honest and stopped me optimizing share copy when
the real gap was evidence.

## Update 3: the fix that actually moved a number (6.5 -> 8.0)

This is the part worth the most to you as proof the loop works. FounderCheck pinned ease of
sale as my lowest dimension (6.5) and was specific about why: I was reading the *3-month
arc* as the *ask*. So I shipped its prescription on-site - made the primary call-to-action
the tiny first ask (one free 30-minute recorded workshop), surfaced the ongoing economics
("keep a cut + Respect + distribution") in "What you walk away with," and added a one-line
objection-killer near the hero (enter at any door). Re-ran it. **Ease of sale went 6.5 ->
8.0, overall 7.2 -> 7.8.**

That is the whole pitch for the tool in one move: it gave a concrete, on-site fix - not a
vibe - I shipped it, and the number moved the predicted direction. The dimensions it could
not move (cold pull-demand, July dropout) it correctly held at TEST FIRST, because those are
data I do not have yet.

## What FounderCheck concretely did for me (the value, named)

1. **Caught a brand-clarity bug from the outside.** The wrong-product BLOCK told me my
   materials misread - before they misread to a human who mattered.
2. **Separated warm-pull from cold-pull.** It made me see that all my proof was warm, and
   that I had been conflating "my community wants this" with "strangers want this." Naming
   which validation I was missing was the unlock.
3. **Designed the cheapest experiment that moves the gate.** 30 cold DMs in 72h, one pass/
   fail number (10 signups), tagged `source: cold-dm` so the data is clean. Smallest test
   that flips the single blocking assumption.
4. **Pointed at the lowest score with a concrete fix.** Ease of sale 6.5 came from reading
   the 3-month arc as the ask; the fix was to surface the tiny first ask (one free workshop)
   and the ongoing economics on-site. That is a funnel change, not a pitch change.
5. **Held the evidence line.** Every time I tried to re-pitch instead of test, it said TEST
   FIRST. Annoying in the moment, correct in hindsight.

## The teaching cut (for your Farcaster Batches workshop)

ZABAL Gamez is a clean worked example for a builder audience. The arc:

1. **A gate is a map, not a verdict.** A BLOCK can be a clarity bug; a RETHINK can be three
   strengths and one untested assumption. Teach reading the *why*, not the number.
2. **Know which validation you are missing.** Warm-pull (your community wants it) is not
   cold-pull (strangers want it). Founders conflate them and either over-claim or panic.
3. **The customer is not always the prize-maximizer.** When the product self-selects for
   meaning and ownership over cash, benchmarking against bigger-prize programs is a category
   error. Segment-fit before competitor-fear.
4. **Currency depends on the customer.** Builders are paid, not paying - so willingness to
   pay is willingness to spend *time*. The RSVP-to-attend rate is the right proxy.
5. **Design the cheapest test that moves the gate.** 30 DMs, one number, clean tags. That is
   the template.
