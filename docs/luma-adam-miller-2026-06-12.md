## Background - who Adam is, what miDAO is
Use this to fill out the listing and to prep the conversation. Confirm specifics
with Adam before publishing anything you are unsure of.

- Adam Miller is the co-founder and CEO of miDAO (MIDAO Directory Services).
  miDAO helps DAOs and online communities set up a legally recognized entity.
- The core idea: most DAOs and communities operate as informal groups, which can
  leave individual members personally exposed and makes it hard to do normal
  things a real organization needs to do. A legal wrapper fixes that.
- What a legal entity unlocks for a community:
  - Limited liability, so members are not personally on the hook for the group's actions.
  - The ability to sign contracts, open accounts, hold funds, and pay vendors as one entity.
  - A clear way to handle taxes, compliance, and off-chain relationships.
  - Legal personhood while keeping on-chain governance and how the group already operates.
- miDAO is known for the Marshall Islands DAO LLC, an entity type written into law
  specifically for DAOs, designed to map to on-chain governance rather than force a
  traditional corporate structure on top of it.
- Why it matters for ZABAL Gamez: builders and digital creators here are spinning up
  communities, token projects, and collectives. The moment one of those starts
  handling real money, partnerships, or members, "who is legally responsible and how
  does this thing sign a contract" becomes a live question. This session is the
  practical answer.

Good questions to seed in the room:
- When does a community actually need a legal entity, and when is it premature?
- How does a Marshall Islands DAO LLC differ from a US LLC or a foundation?
- How does the legal wrapper interact with on-chain governance and a token?
- What does it cost and how long does it take to set one up?
