## Positioning (one sentence)

ZABAL Gamez is The ZAO's 3-month Build-A-Thon - ship something real for the
ecosystem, get a ZAO mentor as your teammate, and earn a place in how The ZAO is run.

## What it is / what it is not

- IS: a creator-onboarding build event for The ZAO. June workshops, July open build
  month, August Finals. Free to join. Any AI harness welcome.
- IS NOT: a SaaS, a subscription tool, a pitch competition, or a weekend hackathon.
- IS NOT: a video-game studio or a game-distribution platform. "Games" is the
  arcade / contest frame ("insert coin"), not the product category. Builders here
  ship tools, mini apps, bots, and dashboards for a music + creator ecosystem -
  not necessarily games at all.

This last point matters: the name plus "vibe coding" can read as *video-game dev* to
an outsider. Every top-of-funnel surface should make the build-event-for-The-ZAO
frame unmistakable in its first sentence.

## Who it is for

Talented Farcaster builders - vibe-coders included, any harness - who want context, a
community to build *for*, real money, and ownership. Not people looking for a
prize-and-goodbye hackathon. They already live on Farcaster, X, and the Build-A-Thon
adjacent scenes (FarHack, the Farcaster Agentic Bootcamp).

## The value exchange (this is what the brand sells)

Builders get:
- Deep ZAO context and a mentor as an embedded teammate (not a judge).
- A share of the $500 USDC pool (sponsored by the ZAO festivals team) plus the 1%
  WaveWarZ trade cut that keeps flowing to the builder after the Finals.
- Respect - The ZAO's soulbound governance signal - earned during the event.
- A commemorative collectible on Magnetiq for everyone who ships.
- ZAO distribution and amplification behind their ship, and a repo they keep.

The ZAO gets:
- Onboarded, vetted builders.
- Real tools built on its rails (Empire Builder, Farcaster, Hats, Bonfire, EAS).
- A permanent workshop library on the Magnetiq portal.
- Ecosystem momentum into ZAOstock and beyond.

## Differentiation

The real comparison set is FarHack, builder bootcamps, and accelerators - NOT itch.io
or Discord.

- 3 months, not a weekend. Mentor pairs, not a solo grind.
- The build IS the application. Working code matters, slides do not.
- You keep the repo, the credit, and ongoing economics.
- You earn governance by showing up - Respect during the event, not just a prize.

## Messaging pillars

1. The build IS the application. (anti-pitch-deck)
2. Bring the build, we bring the team + distribution. (mentor + amplification)
3. Earn governance by showing up. (Respect + ownership)

## Voice reminders

Direct, warm, builder-energy. "We learn it together," not "trust us." Drop articles
when terse helps. "Insert coin" once per surface, max. No emojis. No em dashes. No
"hackathon," no "pitch competition," no "crypto-only" in public copy. See the brand kit.

## How to use this doc

Start every new piece of copy from the one-sentence positioning. Lead with the frame,
hit one or two pillars, point to a single next action (sign up / pick a path / cast on
/zabal). If a line could describe a generic hackathon or a video-game tool, rewrite it.
