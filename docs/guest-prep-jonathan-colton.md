## What FounderCheck is (the one-paragraph version)

FounderCheck is a founder-validation tool: you feed it your product and evidence, and it scores
the idea across a few dimensions, then returns a gate that tells you what to do next rather than
just a number. The dimensions seen in practice: problem severity, customer clarity, reach
strategy, and ease of sale. The gates escalate from BLOCK (the materials read as the wrong
product, or the idea is not ready) to RETHINK (de-risk one assumption) to TEST FIRST (strong,
but one thing must be proven with data before you scale) to GO. Its discipline is that it will
not let you talk a score up - it holds the gate until you run the experiment that proves the
missing assumption. (See `docs/foundercheck-recap-for-jonathan-2026-06-02.md`.)

## The teaching arc Jonathan already drafted (for the room)

From the recap written for him, the lessons that travel best to a ZABAL Gamez builder audience:

1. **A gate is a map, not a verdict.** A BLOCK can be a clarity bug, not a dead idea; a RETHINK
   can be three strengths and one untested assumption. Read the why, not the number.
2. **Know which validation you are missing.** Warm-pull (your community wants it) is not
   cold-pull (strangers want it). Founders conflate them and either over-claim or panic.
3. **The customer is not always the prize-maximizer.** When a product self-selects for meaning
   and ownership over cash, benchmarking against bigger-prize programs is a category error.
4. **Currency depends on the customer.** If your users are builders who are paid, not paying,
   then willingness to pay is willingness to spend time - so RSVP-to-attend is the right proxy.
5. **Design the cheapest test that moves the gate.** Example: 30 cold DMs in 72 hours, one
   pass/fail number, clean tags. Smallest test that flips the single blocking assumption.

## The ZABAL Gamez worked example (have this ready)

Jonathan can walk the room through a real before/after, because ZABAL Gamez ran the loop:

- **Run 1 - 3.35, BLOCK.** Scored ZABAL Gamez as "a SaaS for vibe-coder game distribution" -
  the wrong product. The lesson: the BLOCK was right about the clarity, not the idea. If an
  agent reads your materials as the wrong thing, a human will too.
- **Run 2 - 7.2, RETHINK.** Fed the right product + evidence, it landed three strong dimensions
  (customer clarity 8.0 strongest) and named the one gap: cold pull-demand was unproven.
- **Run 3 - 7.8, TEST FIRST.** Shipping its prescribed on-site fix (surface the tiny first ask
  and the ongoing economics) moved ease of sale 6.5 -> 8.0. It then held at TEST FIRST, refusing
  to go to GO until the cold-demand test runs.
- Net: a +4.45 swing, but the point is that each gate was a specific instruction, and the tool
  held the evidence line. (Full write-up: `docs/foundercheck-recap-for-jonathan-2026-06-02.md`;
  scoring detail: `docs/foundercheck-context-brief-2026-05-29.md`,
  `docs/foundercheck-score-raising-2026-06-02.md`.)

## Talk 2 - propagating your work on Farcaster

The distribution session. Jonathan's lane is getting a shipped thing seen and used on Farcaster
(his work touches Fotocaster, an artist-distribution product where artists keep almost all of
every sale - per the Farcaster Batches Day 1 recap). For a ZABAL Gamez builder this is the
natural follow-on to FounderCheck: validate the idea, then get it in front of people. Good
angles to request: channels and casting cadence that actually move reach, turning a launch into
a repeatable loop, and how the artist-keeps-the-sale model informs creator distribution.
