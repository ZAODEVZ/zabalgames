## What ZABAL Gamez uniquely owns

1. **The only event with a real artist + creator lane.** Almost every event above is dev-only.
   ZABAL Gamez has three tracks (artist, builder, creator) on top of an actual 100+ music/creator
   community. Musicians, visual artists, and media builders have nowhere else in this orbit.
2. **A 3-month arc with three independent doors, not a weekend sprint.** Watch June workshops,
   ship in July (can be a weekend), opt into the August Finals. You can do one door. Competitors
   are mostly time-boxed sprints.
3. **Mentor-as-teammate, not judge.** The strongest builds get a ZAO mentor paired for a build
   window - a collaborator, not a scorer. Structurally different from a hackathon panel.
4. **Ongoing economics + ownership, not a one-time trophy.** A repo you keep, Respect (soulbound
   governance), ZAO distribution, and ongoing on-chain economics where the build plugs into rails
   (e.g. the WaveWarz trade-volume cut). Most programs pay once and you are on your own.
5. **Build on real, composable rails + a community that shows up.** Empire Builder, Bonfire,
   fractal/Respect, /zabal - plus a community with a 100+ week unbroken weekly streak. Distribution
   into a live audience, not a leaderboard you exit.

## Where it is genuinely weaker (be honest)

- **Cash.** $500 v0 is small next to 200 ETH (Onchain Summer) or $500K (Arbitrum). Do not pretend
  otherwise. (Mitigations: the sponsor/grant pipeline in `docs/sponsor-outreach-v1-pool.md`, and
  routing builders to retroactive grants.)
- **Cold pull-demand is unproven.** All current proof is warm (the ZAO orbit). The FounderCheck
  reframe names this honestly; the cold-DM test is the fix, not a louder pitch.
- **Timing overlap.** Onchain Summer runs the same window as our June. That is a threat only if we
  frame it as competition (see sharpener #3).

## Three ways to sharpen the pitch

1. **Lead with the lane no one else owns: artist + creator, on a real music community.**
   Stop opening with "a build event" (it sounds like every other hackathon and, per FounderCheck,
   gets read as the wrong product). Open with the wedge: "the build event for digital creators -
   musicians, artists, and media builders - on Farcaster, run by a 100+ member music community."
   Builders are a track, not the whole story. This is the single clearest differentiator and the
   one competitors cannot copy.

2. **Sell the after, not the during.** Every hackathon sells the sprint and the prize. Sell what
   happens when the event ends: a repo you keep, a crew (not a leaderboard), distribution into a
   live audience, Respect, and economics that keep paying. Name the problem it solves out loud -
   the post-hackathon ghost town ("I shipped at a hackathon and then nothing happened"). The pitch
   is "a crew, not a leaderboard," and it directly answers the cold-builder's real fear.

3. **Reframe the cash gap, and ride the big events instead of fighting them.** Do not compete on
   pool size. Two moves: (a) position depth over cash - 3 months, a mentor-teammate, real
   distribution beat a bigger one-time check; (b) explicitly co-exist with the big pools - a
   ZABAL Gamez July build can also be submitted to Onchain Summer / FarHack / a retroactive Base
   grant. "Build it here with a crew and mentors, then take it everywhere the money is" turns the
   overlap from a threat into a feature.
