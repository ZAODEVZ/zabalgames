## Description
```
A deep, candid tour of the Farcaster protocol from Cassie (Cassandra Heart) - founder of Quilibrium and a contributor to Farcaster. What "sufficiently decentralized" really meant, the hubs era and its scaling limits, the rewrite into Snapchain, and the Hypersnap fork.

In this builder-track ZABAL Gamez session, Cassie lays out the two centralization problems - the validator set, controlled mostly by one provider, and the fact that you cannot build a real client straight off Snapchain - then walks through Hypersnap as an additive side chain: threshold signing, the Snap token and gasless bridging, consensus and one-second blocks, the external OP Mainnet contracts (ID, storage, key, and FNAME registries), gasless signers, spam and trust scoring, and the roadmap.

She closes with the five North Stars for getting Farcaster to a million daily actives - chief among them, making it possible to build a client in a weekend.

What you'll learn:
- What "sufficiently decentralized" meant, and where it fell short
- Why you cannot build a real client straight off Snapchain
- How Hypersnap works as an additive side chain
- The external OP Mainnet contracts every builder should know
- Gasless signers, the Snap token, and the bridge
- The roadmap and the five North Stars to a million daily actives
```
