## Title
ZABAL GAMEZ Workshop - Owning Your Music as an Independent Artist - w/ JOSEPH GOATS

## Description
```
Joseph Goats (Jose Cabrera) built a whole music life out of internet tools - in a country with no music industry to plug into, and in 2017, not even a bank card. This is his story, and his model: digital street music.

In this artist-track ZABAL Gamez session, Jose breaks down what "digital street music" means - he plays for free, peer-to-peer, so anyone anywhere can support him directly. He traces the journey from discovering writing and online contests on Steemit (later Hive), to learning English along the way, to running impact concerts and reimagining festivals for social impact and education.

It is a wide-ranging, hopeful talk on decentralization as a fractal - everyone has a fingerprint and something to say - on the artist as a bard who travels between communities and connects them, and on aligning music with nature and biology. He closes by playing a song.

What you'll take away:
- Digital street music: playing free and peer-to-peer so anyone can support you directly
- Why fragmentation is the real problem decentralization has to solve
- How he entered through writing and online contests, and watched a community fork and keep going
- The artist as a bard and a node - turning a peer-to-peer connection into a real-world tour or festival
- Aligning music with nature and biology (Humberto Maturana, autopoiesis)
- Reimagining festivals for social impact and education

In his own words, it is not about any buzzword - it is about how internet tools today can benefit you.
```
